04
Upgrade

Upgrading a patient's dosage

When a dosage needs to change, you edit the Subscription itself — not an individual order. Remove the old variation, add the new one, hit Recalculate, then Update. Next renewal clones from the new state, at the new price. (That's exactly how order #116118 back in Step 2 ended up at £175.74.)

Golden rule. Edits here (on the Subscription page) affect all future renewals.

07
For testing only

Testing toolkit

Two shortcuts to make testing painless. Once the plugin is live and stable, these become background tricks rather than daily tools — but during the testing window, they save you a month of waiting.

Skip the 1-month wait

Force a renewal to fire in 1 hour instead of a month.

On any subscription's admin page, find the Schedule box on the right.

  1. In the Next Payment field, set the time (the HH and MM boxes) to a moment within the next hour — e.g. if it's 14:23 now, type 14:55.
  2. Click the date field. The calendar popup defaults to one month ahead — click the ‹ arrow to go back to the current month.
  3. Pick today's date. A browser popup will appear: "Please enter a date at least one hour into the future."
  4. Click OK. The field auto-snaps to exactly one hour from now.
  5. Click Update at the top of the subscription page.
Result: the renewal fires in 1 hour instead of waiting for the real renewal date. Patient still receives the reminder email; the rest of the flow plays out as production.

That's it

Three things to remember

If you come away with these, you've got the plugin.

Edit the Subscription, not the order

Dosage changes live on the subscription record. Next renewal clones from there.

Patients get seven days' notice

An email and a dashboard prompt land 7 days before renewal. Adjust anytime in Settings → Subscriptions.

"Missing Consultation" is your cue

Filter the orders page for it. Contact the patient, point them to their dashboard, refund if they've gone quiet.
